# Structure

**The revised structure:** Chapter-based progression instead of one 30-day countdown. Each chapter has its own deadline + vehicle tier + unlocked zone. Clearing a chapter unlocks the next one — no dead end, no confusion about "what now."

## What stays, what changes
* **✅ Opening premise**: Family loan, debt deadline, pressure to earn. Keeps the emotional hook without needing deep story or named NPCs.
* **✅ Random passengers**: Spawn at random places and times. No names, no backstory. Just type (tourist, worker, student etc) and a fare. Clean and simple.
* **✅ 30-day deadline**: Kept — but now it's per chapter. Clear Chapter 1 in 30 days → unlock Chapter 2 with a new deadline. The pressure never disappears.
* **🔄 "What happens after debt?"**: You move to the next chapter with a bigger goal, a new vehicle tier, and a new unlocked zone. Progress feels continuous, not like starting over.
* **🔄 NPCs simplified**: Mechanic is a shop screen, not a character. Moneylender is just a HUD countdown + notification. No dialogue trees needed.
* **➕ End-of-day screen**: After each day: earnings, expenses, vehicle condition, optional mechanic visit. Then new day starts.
* **➕ Shop**: One currency (NPR) for everything — vehicles, upgrades, new zones, mechanic repairs. Player decides what to prioritise.

## The core tension — one currency, three competing needs
Every NPR earned has to be split between:
**1. Paying the debt** — mandatory, has a deadline
**2. Upgrading the vehicle** — earns more per ride, but costs money now
**3. Unlocking new zones** — more passengers, better fares, but upfront cost

This is the core decision loop. Do you save for the debt or invest in growth? Investing in a vehicle upgrade before clearing the debt is a genuine risk — but it might be the right move.

# Chapters

## Chapter progression — each one is a self-contained challenge

### Chapter 1: The Loan
*Starting out in Thamel*
* **Deadline:** 30 days
* **Target:** NPR 1,00,000
* **Starting vehicle:** 100cc Bajaj bike
* **Zone:** Thamel only
* **Passengers:** 1 at a time
* **Unlock on clear:** Ring Road zone + Maruti 800

### Chapter 2: Expanding
*The city opens up*
* **Deadline:** 45 days
* **Target:** NPR 3,00,000
* **Vehicles available:** Bajaj, Maruti 800
* **Zones:** Thamel + Ring Road
* **Passengers:** Up to 2 (car)
* **Unlock on clear:** Airport corridor + Hyundai i10

### Chapter 3: The Big City
*Airport runs, VIP passengers*
* **Deadline:** 60 days
* **Target:** NPR 7,00,000
* **Vehicles available:** All cars unlocked
* **Zones:** Full city + Airport
* **Passengers:** All types + VIP
* **Unlock on clear:** Pokhara map + EV SUV

### Chapter 4: Beyond Kathmandu
*Endgame — new city, hardest difficulty*
* **Deadline:** 60 days
* **Target:** NPR 20,00,000
* **Vehicles available:** All + Electric SUV
* **Zones:** Pokhara map
* **Passengers:** All types, tougher events
* **Unlock on clear:** Credits + infinite mode

## What happens if you fail a chapter?
Restart that chapter only — not the whole game. You keep your vehicle and any permanent upgrades bought from the shop. Only the cash progress resets. This removes the brutal full-restart frustration while keeping the deadline pressure real.

# Vehicles

## Vehicle tier system
Vehicles are bought from the shop with the same NPR used to pay debt. This is the core tension — upgrade now and earn faster, or save for the deadline. Each tier unlocks more passengers, faster speed, and new zone access.

* **🛵 100cc Bajaj**: 1 passenger, Fast in gallis, Low fuel cost, Cheap repairs | Starting vehicle
* **🏍️ Pulsar 150**: 1 passenger, Faster speed, Better highway, Medium fuel | NPR 40,000
* **🚗 Maruti 800**: 2 passengers, City-wide access, AC = +tip, Higher repair cost | NPR 1,20,000
* **🚕 Hyundai i10**: 3 passengers, Unlocks VIP pool, +30% tip (summer), Airport access | NPR 2,80,000
* **🚐 Microbus**: 6 passengers, Festival events, Slow in gallis, Highest income | NPR 5,00,000
* **⚡ Electric SUV**: 4 passengers, No fuel cost, Eco karma bonus, Everest route | NPR 8,00,000

## Bikes vs cars — key differences
* **🛵 Bikes**: Only 1 passenger. Access narrow gallis cars can't enter. Cheaper fuel, cheaper repairs. Best early game — lower risk, faster around Thamel.
* **🚗 Cars**: 2-4 passengers = higher fare per trip. Airport runs unlocked. VIP passengers only accept cars. Can't enter narrow gallis — must use main roads.
* **Key tradeoff**: Bikes are safer and nimbler. Cars earn more per ride but cost more to run. Upgrading too early (before clearing Ch.1 debt) is a real risk — repair costs can spiral.

# Shop

## Shop — one screen, everything in one place
Accessed from the end-of-day screen. Single currency (NPR). Four categories. Everything competes for the same money — that tension IS the game.

### 🚗 Vehicles
* **🏍️ Pulsar 150**: Faster bike, better highway performance | NPR 40,000
* **🚗 Maruti 800**: First car. 2 passengers. Unlocks Ring Road zone. | NPR 1,20,000
* **🚕 Hyundai i10**: Premium car. VIP pool + airport access. | NPR 2,80,000

### 🗺️ Zone unlocks
* **🔁 Ring Road zone**: Wider roads, more passengers, longer rides | Free on Ch.2
* **✈️ Airport corridor**: Highest single-fare routes in the game | NPR 80,000
* **🏛️ Patan heritage zone**: Tourists + elderly passengers, karma routes | NPR 50,000
* **🏔️ Nagarkot highway**: Long-distance mountain route. High fare, high fuel cost. | NPR 1,50,000

### ⚙️ Vehicle upgrades
* **⛽ Fuel efficiency kit**: −20% fuel consumption permanently | NPR 15,000
* **🔧 Reinforced suspension**: Pothole damage −40%. Useful for Ch.1 Thamel roads. | NPR 20,000
* **❄️ AC unit**: +15% tip in summer months | NPR 25,000
* **📱 GPS / app integration**: Shows passenger tip potential before accepting | NPR 30,000

### 🛡️ Insurance & misc
* **🛡️ Weekly insurance**: Covers one accident/breakdown per week | NPR 5,000/week
* **🎨 Vehicle decoration**: Seasonal decor (Dashain marigold etc) = +5% mood | NPR 2,000

# Mechanic screen

## End-of-day screen flow
After each in-game day ends, the player sees a simple summary screen. From here they can visit the mechanic, visit the shop, or just start the next day. No forced interactions — everything is optional except the debt clock ticking.
